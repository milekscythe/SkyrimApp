About Us

The Shi’hing School of Kung Fu was established in January 2003 after the closure of the Whiston School of Hung Gar Kung Fu ran by the late Sifu Eddie Pugh.

The Shi’hing School of Kung Fu trains using Tian Kai Kung Fu.  Tian Kai uses Hung Gar techniques, but has adapted some ideals to ensure the techniques used are “street smart” and to be able to be used in difficult situations.

Tian Kai is a relatively new style of Kung Fu which is based on the Hung Gar syllabus.  Hung Gar is one of the most famous and respected system of Chinese Martial Arts to emerge out of southern China with roots dating back to the 17th Century Shaolin Temples.  It is a complete system of Kung Fu known for its direct and powerful, no-nonsense martial techniques, self-defence and tremendous benefits.

Besides the obvious and well known benefits, such as self-defence, physical, mental and health benefits, correct Kung Fu training generates positive results in every aspect of life – better focus, motivation, increased self-confidence, self-discipline, stress management, calmness under pressure, strength of character and more.

The training at our school includes:

  • Fundamental exercises
  • Solo, partner and group drills
  • Traditional hand and leg forms
  • Traditional weapons forms
  • Combat sequences and sparring
  • Application drills and self-defence
  • Strengthening and conditioning

The school offers a relaxed training atmosphere, and as we are mainly a family based school, the classes are enjoyable and are very suitable for beginners to martial arts.  Teaching is at the students’ own pace, and we will never force you to perform a technique that you are not ready for.

The school does not allow potential students or visitors to spectate during the classes.  I have often found that potential students who watch do not return.  Their thoughts are often “I won’t be able to do that…” whereas they hadn’t even attempted the technique.  95% of students who attend and join in the very first lesson will return and become members of the school.  The other 5% feel that this style of martial arts is just not for them.

School Information

Our doors are open to new students for ages 7 upwards (children and adults) at all times.  You are more than welcome to attend one of the classes.

A new student pays on a class-by-class basis for the first 4 weeks.  For junior students (ages 7-15), the cost is £4.50 per class.  For senior students (ages 16+), the class fees are £5.50.

After the fourth week, you will be required to purchase a membership package.

For junior students, the membership package costs £50; a senior package costs £60.  The membership package includes your training uniform, your licence and your insurance.  The school uniform consists of a school t-shirt (juniors wear a grey t-shirt with the school emblem; seniors wear a black t-shirt with the school emblem and include a Chinese training name personalised on the back), a pair of cuffed Kung Fu trousers in black and a white sash.

Class fees will then be paid on a 4-weekly basis in advance.

If you are under 16 years old, you will need a parent or guardian to bring you to complete the application form.  They will need to sign and date this form.

Our classes are held of a Monday and Thursday, starting at 7pm and ending at 8:30pm.  As the class starts at 7pm, all students must attend a minimum of 5 minutes prior to class commencement.
